 Day 10. Philosophy. Pay attention to the words you habitually use. A word of caution. Select your words wisely. There are so many ways to describe something, and each one will alter how you feel about it. For example, if you lose your wallet, you probably won't be too happy about it. Imagine telling your friend, I'm so angry I could tear somebody to shreds. That would make you angrier than you originally were. Imagine instead that you said, I'm a little upset about it. You wouldn't feel as bad, would you? So, use less intense words when things upset you. On the other hand, you can use this technique for positive things too. If you had some great pastor, you could say, that penne was out of this world. You'd feel even better than if you had said, that penne was pretty good. This holds true for any language you're using too. Remember, the stronger your language, the stronger you feel. Therefore, you should be very careful with the words you habitually use.
