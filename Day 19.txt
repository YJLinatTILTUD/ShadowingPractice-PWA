 Day 19. Self-care. Let go of regrets. Getting rid of regret is like clearing old clutter from your room. It frees up space for new, positive experiences. Holding onto regrets is like carrying a backpack full of stones. It only weighs you down. A good way to overcome regret is to turn it into a learning experience and resolve to change what you're doing starting now. A common regret people have is that they focus too much on work but not enough on family. Ask yourself, have I fallen into this trap? If you have, then pull out your calendar immediately and schedule regular family time so that you can avoid suffering this regret in the future. You know that family is critically important, so be sure to make it a part of your regular schedule. Whatever you do, though, don't dwell on the regret. Instead, work to ensure that you never experience this exact same regret again. Transforming regret into a lesson and action helps you grow and move forward. Regret no longer.
